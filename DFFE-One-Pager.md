# 🎣 FishTrack Africa - Partnership Proposal for DFFE

**Citizen Science Platform for Recreational Fishing Data Collection**

---

## What is FishTrack Africa?

FishTrack Africa is a **free mobile web app** that allows South African recreational fishers to log their catches and share fishing data in real-time. It's a community-driven platform designed to support sustainable fishing practices while building a comprehensive database of recreational fishing activity across South Africa and Namibia.

**Live Platform:** https://fishtrack-sa.netlify.app  
**Launch Date:** March 2026  
**Coverage:** South African coastline + inland dams, Namibian coast

---

## What Data We Collect

Every time a user logs a catch, FishTrack captures:

- ✅ **Species** (45 species database: saltwater + freshwater)
- ✅ **GPS Location** (with privacy controls for secret spots)
- ✅ **Date & Time** of catch
- ✅ **Weight/Length** (with built-in calculator)
- ✅ **Environmental Conditions** (tide, wind, water temperature)
- ✅ **Photo Evidence** (optional but encouraged)
- ✅ **Fishing Method** (shore, boat, kayak, etc.)

---

## Why This Matters for DFFE

### Current Challenge:
DFFE issues ±300,000 recreational fishing permits annually, generating R18M in revenue. However, **limited data exists** on:
- What species recreational fishers actually catch
- Where catches occur (regional distribution)
- When catches happen (seasonal trends)
- Size distribution of recreational catches

### How FishTrack Helps:

**Real-Time Monitoring:**
- Track recreational fishing activity across all coastal and inland regions
- Identify fishing hotspots and pressure points
- Monitor compliance with bag limits and size restrictions

**Stock Assessment Support:**
- Citizen science data complements DFFE's scientific surveys
- Fill gaps in recreational catch data (currently estimated at 17,000 tons/year)
- Early warning system for declining species populations

**Evidence-Based Policy:**
- Data-driven decisions on quotas, bag limits, and closed seasons
- Regional insights (Western Cape vs. Eastern Cape vs. KZN)
- Freshwater and marine fisheries combined

## Partnership Opportunity

### What DFFE Gets:
- ✅ **Free access** to anonymized recreational fishing data (quarterly reports)
- ✅ **Citizen engagement** - fishers actively participating in conservation
- ✅ **Cost savings** - no need to build/maintain government platform
- ✅ **Compliance insights** - identify areas of overfishing or illegal activity

### What FishTrack Asks:
- ✅ **Endorsement** - list FishTrack on DFFE website as recommended data platform
- ✅ **Promotion** - mention FishTrack in recreational fishing pamphlets and permits
- ✅ **Data reciprocity** - share stock assessment reports with FishTrack (public info)
- ✅ **Collaboration** - quarterly meetings to review data and align on priorities

### No Cost to Government:
FishTrack is privately funded and operates at no expense to DFFE.

## Privacy & Compliance

**User Privacy:**
- All data shared with DFFE is **anonymized** (no user names or contact info)
- GPS coordinates can be fuzzed to protect "secret spots"
- POPIA compliant (SA privacy law)

**Data Ownership:**
- FishTrack retains ownership of platform
- DFFE receives non-exclusive license to use aggregated data
- No commercial use without FishTrack approval

## Pilot Program Proposal

### Phase 1 (3 Months):
- FishTrack provides sample data from Western Cape (highest activity)
- Focus species: West Coast Rock Lobster, Galjoen, line fish
- Quarterly report delivered to DFFE Fisheries Management

### Phase 2 (6 Months):
- Expand to all coastal provinces
- Add freshwater catches (dams, rivers)
- Joint press release: "DFFE partners with FishTrack for citizen science"

### Phase 3 (12 Months):
- Formal MOU signed
- FishTrack promoted to all 300,000 permit holders
- Integration with permit renewal process (optional data upload)

## Why Now?

**Declining Stocks:**
DFFE's 2024 Status Report confirms:
- Abalone: "heavily depleted"
- Anchovy: lowest level in 15 years
- Line fish: many species "heavily depleted"
- Unknown status for many species (limited data)

**Better data = better decisions.**

FishTrack offers a scalable, cost-free solution to monitor recreational fishing and support sustainable resource management.
